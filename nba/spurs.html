About History

Overview

The San Antonio Spurs are an American professional basketball team that represents the city of San Antonio, Texas, in the NBA. The team plays its home games at Frost Bank Center in San Antonio. It plays in the Southwest Group of the Western Conference in the NBA. The franchise has won NBA championships in 1999, 2003, 2005, 2007, and 2014

The team started out as the Dallas Chaparrals in the American Basketball Association (ABA). The Chaparrals were moderately successful, but the team was sold to a group of San Antonio businessmen in 1973, relocated, and renamed the Spurs. George (“the Iceman”) Gervin—a future Hall of Famer who joined the franchise midway through the 1973–74 season—was the star of the early San Antonio teams, a high-scoring shooting guard who would help establish the Spurs as a consistent contender throughout the 1970s and 1980s.

The Dynasty

The word "dynasty" has often been used to describe the run San Antonio had from 1999 until 2014, in which it won five NBA championships in 15 seasons to go along with three Western Conference Finals appearances and another NBA Finals showing. Some have even taken it a step further to include their spot in the 2017 Western Conference Finals.

+'s and -'s

Spurs have a tradition of effective offensive play. Quarterbacks such as Tony Parker and later Dejounte Murray were successful in organizing offenses. Their success, especially in outside shots, enabled them to control their game. Ball sharing and passing traffic as a team is one of the keys to Spurs' offensive performance. Their system aims to create open space and find the best shot. Spurs have a tradition of defending as a team. Emphasizes systems, rotations, and cooperation. They have an effective collective defense approach to create difficult shots against their opponents. Aside from players like Victor Wembanyama, rim defense is one of the Spurs' strengths. They position well, block and are effective in defensive rebounds.
